In online discussions, a set of seven criticisms often surfaces — claiming that Indian Muslims are economically broken, that the country is driven by hatred, and that democracy is in peril. These assertions make for compelling sound bites, but they collapse when measured against official data, independent global surveys, and the lived experience of 1.4 billion citizens. Here is a point-by-point examination.

### 1. “Why are Muslims economically so weak?”

The question itself relies on a sweeping generalisation. Poverty exists in every community, yet the narrative ignores the full picture. India is home to Muslim billionaires like **Azim Premji** (~$11 billion) and **M.A. Yusuff Ali** (~$4.5 billion), and a fast-growing Muslim middle class.

Crucially, all central welfare schemes are religion‑blind — and the numbers prove it.

- **14% of Ujjwala free‑LPG beneficiaries are Muslim**, almost exactly matching the community’s 14.2% population share.  
- **15% of PMAY‑Urban houses are reserved for minorities**, with allotment on a first‑come, first‑served basis.  
- **Multidimensional poverty fell from 24.85% (2015‑16) to 14.96% (2019‑21)** per NITI Aayog, lifting all communities — Muslims included — in equal measure.

Economic weakness is therefore not a faith‑specific phenomenon but a developmental challenge being addressed across the board.

### 2. “The majority gets the dopamine of hatred. Tyrants facilitate riots. Destroying democracy.”

A government securing a massive mandate does so on the back of tangible delivery, not manufactured hatred. The data on communal harmony tells a compelling story.

- **Communal riot incidents dropped from 644 (2014) to just 378 (2022)** — a **41% decline** (National Crime Records Bureau).  
- **65% of Indians say they are satisfied with democratic functioning** (Pew Research Center, 2021), higher than the global median.  
- **Morning Consult pegs Prime Minister Modi’s approval at 75‑78%**, the highest globally, driven by record welfare reach and economic reforms — not animosity.

Democracy is being reinforced by performance legitimacy, not undercut by it.

### 3. “BJP is facilitating hatred against Muslims.”

The party’s governing slogan — *Sabka Saath, Sabka Vikas, Sabka Vishwas* — translates into budgets and outcomes, not divisive rhetoric.

- The **Minority Affairs budget rose by 83%** from ₹2,727 crore (2014‑15) to ₹5,020 crore (2022‑23).  
- **Muslim student enrolment in higher education climbed from 4.4% to 5.5%** (AISHE 2020‑21).  
- **Approximately 14% of PM‑KISAN income‑support beneficiaries are Muslim** (Lok Sabha reply), once again mirroring the population share.  
- Hate speech is prosecuted under existing law; the government does not sponsor it.

When every flagship programme reaches Muslims proportionally, the charge of systemic hatred crumbles.

### 4. “Manipur violence = tribalism.”

The Manipur conflict is a long‑standing ethnic dispute over land, identity, and resources between Meiteis and Kukis, with roots stretching back decades — including the 1993 Kuki‑Naga clashes. Pinpointing the Prime Minister for a deep‑seated regional conflict ignores history and local agency. The central government has deployed **over 50,000 paramilitary personnel** and is actively facilitating peace‑building dialogue. Tribal tensions are not a new invention of 2023, and solutions require nuanced local engagement, not simplistic national scapegoating.

### 5. “Why choose a leader who asks to hate others?”

This framing completely misreads the Prime Minister’s public messaging, which centres on “New India”, women’s empowerment, and self‑reliance (*Aatmanirbhar Bharat*). The electoral reward flows from visible, life‑changing delivery:

- **50+ crore Jan Dhan bank accounts** opened, bringing the unbanked into the financial mainstream.  
- **11+ crore toilets built**, dramatically improving sanitation and dignity.  
- **3+ crore houses sanctioned** under PMAY.  
- **Free rations for 80 crore people during COVID** — the world’s largest food security programme.  

**78% of Indians approve of PM Modi’s leadership** (Morning Consult, 2024) because they see these results in their daily lives. People vote for development, not hate.

### 6. “Making Muslims second‑class citizens.”

Constitutionally impossible and factually false. Muslims occupy some of the highest offices in the land.

- **Justice S. Abdul Nazeer served on the Supreme Court (2017‑2023)**; Muslim scientists, IAS, and IPS officers excel at every level.  
- **72% of Indian Muslims say they are free to practice their religion** (Gallup, 2023).  
- Every government benefit — Ujjwala, PMAY, PM‑KISAN, Jan Dhan — is equally available. No discriminatory law has been passed. Muslims vote in massive numbers, enjoy full legal protection, and are co‑equal citizens in the world’s largest democracy.

### 7. “After 15 years of power, supporters are stupid or support hatred.”

This is an elitist dismissal of the voter’s intelligence. People reward performance, and the performance is formidable.

- **India’s GDP more than doubled** from ~$1.86 trillion (2014) to ~$3.7 trillion (2023).  
- **Extreme poverty (<$2.15/day) fell from 18.7% (2015) to under 2% (2021)** per World Bank data — an unprecedented decline.  
- **13.5 crore Indians exited multidimensional poverty** between 2015‑16 and 2019‑21 (NITI Aayog).  

The driving ideology is “Nation First” with inclusive development. Labeling crores of fellow citizens as stupid or hateful is not just factually incorrect — it is the real prejudice.

---

**Bottom line:** Before accepting facile narratives, it’s worth checking the data. India’s story — for Muslims and all citizens alike — is one of rapid poverty reduction, expanding democratic satisfaction, and access to welfare that doesn’t ask about religion.
